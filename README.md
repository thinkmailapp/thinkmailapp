## The story

A college student. Too many emails. Not enough time — or patience — to reply to all of them well.

The usual routine was opening ChatGPT in one tab, copying the email thread into it, writing a prompt, getting a generic reply that sounded nothing like her, and then editing it anyway. Every single time. For every single email.

She wasn't looking for a grammar checker. She wasn't looking for a tool that would make her sound more "professional" in the abstract. She wanted something that actually **read the situation** — who this person was, what had been said before, what the tone of the conversation was — and helped her respond in a way that made sense for that specific moment.

She also didn't want to sound rude to her professors, colleagues, or employers when she was just trying to be brief. Or come across as too casual when the thread had quietly turned serious. Or miss something someone said three emails back that she definitely should have addressed.

So she built ThinkMail. Right inside Gmail. No tab switching. No copy-pasting. No prompting from scratch. Just open the thread, click one button, and get a reply that actually fits.

---

## What it does

**⚡ Think about this email**

Click once. ThinkMail reads your full Gmail thread — every message, not just the last one — and returns:

- **Tone · Urgency · Vibe · Intent · Risk** — understand the situation at a glance before you type a word
- **Situation breakdown** — plain English summary of what's actually going on in this thread
- **Context analysis** — power dynamics, tone shifts, what's been promised, what's gone unsaid
- **Conflict detection** — what your draft is missing or contradicting from earlier in the thread
- **Suggested reply** — written to match the exact format of your email history with this person. If you two always end emails with "Thanks, [name]" — it does that. If it's a casual thread with no sign-off — it does that too.

**Already typed something in the reply box?**

ThinkMail reads your draft too. Even if it's just a few words or a short aggressive line you typed in frustration — it takes what you meant to say, understands the situation, and rewrites it in the right tone for that relationship. Your intent, better delivered.

---

## Why it's different

The problem with every other AI email tool is that they fix **how** you write, not **what** you write. You get a polished version of a reply that might still be completely wrong for the situation.

ThinkMail asks a different question first: is this the right thing to say, to this person, in this moment?

It catches things like:
- You sound too apologetic when the other person is actually at fault
- You're ignoring something they brought up two emails ago
- Your tone is casual but the thread just became urgent
- You're being too blunt with someone you actually need a favour from
- Your draft is fine but your sign-off doesn't match how you've always closed with this person

## Privacy

Your emails are your business — not ours.

- **Your email content is never stored.** It's processed in real time to generate a response and immediately discarded. Nothing is written to disk or any database.
- **No Gmail API access.** ThinkMail reads directly from the Gmail page in your browser. We never request permission to read, send, or modify your emails.
- **We only store** your name, email address, and a daily usage count. That's it.
